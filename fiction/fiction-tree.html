Dune

by Frank Herbert

Dune by Frank Herbert, first published in 1965, is a seminal work in science fiction. Set in a distant future, the novel explores the struggle for control over the desert planet Arrakis, also known as Dune. Arrakis is the only source of the essential substance "spice," which is vital for space travel and grants extended life. The story follows Paul Atreides, the young heir of House Atreides, as his family is assigned to govern Arrakis. After a betrayal leads to their downfall, Paul and his mother, Lady Jessica, seek refuge with the Fremen, the native people of Arrakis. Paul discovers his potential as a messianic figure with extraordinary abilities. Dune explores themes of power, religion, and ecology, delving into how political and religious forces shape societies.
